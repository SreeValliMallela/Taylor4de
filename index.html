    WHY I'M RUNNING

    Middletown Deserves a Voice That Listens

    Thank you for visiting my website. Campaigning for an elected office was not on my bingo card for 2026. The closest I've come to politics is serving our community's HOA president for the past 15 years. In our community, when an issue arises, we work together to resolve it in the best interests of our community.

    However, I could no longer sit on the sidelines while decisions made in Dover continued to negatively impact our families, small businesses, and public safety here in Middletown. I decided to step forward because we can do better than we are.

    If elected, I intend to do what is right, not what is politically convenient. I do not claim to have all the answers. However, I will listen, seek expertise, study the issues thoroughly, and make decisions based on what is best for Middletown with your input.

    My commitment is simple: to understand the issues, explain them clearly, and vote in the best interest of our community.My commitment is simple: to understand the issues, explain them clearly, and vote in the best interest of our Middletown community.

    ABOUT THE CANDIDATE

    A Lifelong Delawarean Fighting for You

    I am 57 years old and have lived in Delaware for 56 of those years. I have proudly called Middletown home for nearly 30 years, and during that time, I have witnessed tremendous growth and change. Some of those changes have been positive; others have not. Like many of you, I care deeply about preserving what makes our community strong while addressing what needs to improve.

    My wife, Michelle, and I have been together for 40 years. We raised our three children, Jordan, Ryan, and Marissa, right here in Middletown. Michelle and I both attended Caravel Academy and graduated from the University of Delaware. I later earned a Master's Degree in Education from Wilmington University.

    All three of our children attended public schools in the Appoquinimink School District and graduated from Appoquinimink High School. Our oldest daughter, Jordan, has Down syndrome, which has given our family firsthand experience navigating Individualized Education Programs (IEPs) and understanding the educational and employment challenges faced by individuals with disabilities.

    Ryan and Marissa both continued their education at the University of Delaware and graduated with honors.

    I have spent many years volunteering in our community, as a coach for youth sports, including soccer with Delaware Union, baseball with Bear Babe Ruth, and bocce with the MOT Tigers Special Olympics. I also volunteer at our church, LifeHouse, and at various community events. Serving others has been both personally and professionally rewarding.

    Professional Experience

    I spent 20 years in law enforcement with the New Castle County Police Department, where I served in both operational and leadership roles. I was the first full-time assigned detective for Middletown, back in the late 90s, before the town had its own police department.

    I retired as a Senior Sergeant to become a Criminal Investigator with the Delaware Department of Justice. After three years conducting criminal investigations, I was appointed Director of the Victim Witness Assistance Program, overseeing victim services statewide.

    A core responsibility of my work has been ensuring that victims' voices are heard and respected throughout the prosecution process. Advocacy, accountability, and fairness have guided my entire career.

    With your support, I hope to bring that same commitment to representing Middletown in Legislative Hall because I believe our district has not had as strong a voice at the table as it deserves.

    "My family is why I'm running. Every decision I make will be measured by how it affects families like ours."

    THE ISSUES

    How I Will Vote on Legislation

    I will vote yes or no based on the merits of each bill and its impact on Middletown, not based on party labels or political pressure.

    We deserve transparency, not backroom deals or partisan games. My guiding principle will always be:

    "Does this make sense for us in Middletown?"

    This campaign is not about parties, it is about solutions and results. Constant fighting and finger-pointing do nothing for taxpayers, homeowners, renters, or small business owners. We need action that produces measurable, positive outcomes.

    WHY NOW

    It's About Principles, Not Politics

    Delaware has been under one-party control for nearly 40 years. Voters must ask themselves: Are we better off today? Is the current direction working?

    Democrats are one seat away in the House from a constitutional supermajority, which would give them full authority to amend the Delaware Constitution, impacting religious freedom, gun rights, property rights, and voting protections.

    I am running because Delaware needs balance, accountability, and dialogue—not one-party rule.

    Balance & Accountability

    One-party rule leads to complacency. Delaware needs representatives who will ask tough questions and demand accountability.

    Common-Sense Over Ideology

    I'm not interested in partisan warfare. I'll evaluate every bill on its merits, asking: 'Does this make sense for Middletown?' That's the only test that matters.

    Fiscal Responsibility

    Government should live within its means, just like families do. Before asking for more from taxpayers, we should ensure every dollar is being spent wisely.

    Individual Freedom

    Parents should have a voice in their children's education. Business owners should be able to operate without excessive regulation. Government should empower, not control.

    "I will vote on the merits of every issue. My only loyalty is to the people of Middletown and to common sense."
